Guest Lecture (ICT108): "Talk to Your Documents" — Building a Real-World AI Assistant

This one-hour session walks through how a real AI product is designed and built, using a document question-answering chatbot as the running example. It is a case study of an actual system rather than a coding tutorial, focused on the concepts and the engineering decisions behind them.

The session begins with a familiar idea: dropping a PDF into a tool like ChatGPT and asking questions. It then examines why this does not scale to thousands of documents or to users without a paid subscription, and builds a document chatbot step by step. At each step the naive system fails in an instructive way, and the failure motivates the concept that resolves it.

Across the journey students encounter the core ideas behind modern AI systems: embeddings (representing meaning as numbers), retrieval and RAG (locating the right passage), hallucination (why models answer confidently but incorrectly), and reliability (making a system trustworthy). Each concept is grounded in a real problem observed while building the system.
